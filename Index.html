Christopher Thorn Arme

Game Designer and Programmer

Hello. My name is Chris.

I like to make things: games, code, art, music, laughter. ScuidCode is my home on the Interwebs.

I'm a full-time college student at SMU, majoring in Creative Computation
as part of The Meadows Scholars Program.

Before completing high school in 2017 with a 4.0 GPA, I attended Collin College as part of a dual-enrollment program. At Collin College I studied Japanese, Drawing, and Symbolic Logic.

I have studied guitar (classical and electric) for over 10 years. In 2015, I took first place in the Mountain View College guitar festival competition, senior division. I have also taught guitar and participated as a member of the guitar orchestra at Brookhaven College.

In 2014, my team and I were world finalists in Odyssey of the Mind after placing in regional and state competitions. We placed 15th in the world and won a Ranatra Fusca Award for outstanding creativity. I continue to participate in Odyssey of the Mind as a regional and state judge.

Aquarium

You like fish? I like fish (and squids).

Written in Processing, this demo is primarily an exercise in object-oriented programming. The actors in the scene are procedurally generated, so you'll never get exactly the same view. The fish-bobbing, bubble-jiggling, and weed-swaying are all calculated using a sine function.

Websites

Himes Service Company

Lead Designer, Programmer, Project Manager

The Summer after high school, I designed and implemented a web site for Himes Service Company. This was my first time designing a web site from scratch. I worked with the customer to define requirements, I produced the initial mock-ups for the web site, and I worked with a team of developers to implement the site. This web site is fully optimized for mobile.

The site was written in HTML, JavaScript, and CSS using Visual Studio 2017. The platform is .Net core 1.1 and ASP.NET Core MVC. Various libraries were incorporated including jQuery, Bootstrap, and Font Awesome. Other tools used include Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, and git.

You can take a look at my last round of mock-ups here.
